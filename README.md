# Power-BI-Telecon-churn-Rate-Dashboard
Telecom Customer Churn Analysis Power BI dashboard analyzing churn patterns across 7,043 customers  to identify high-risk segments and drive data-backed retention strategy.

Questions Asked
- What is the overall churn rate and how many customers are at risk?
- Which contract type has the highest churn and by how much?
- Does internet service type influence churn behavior?
- How does customer tenure impact likelihood of churn?
- Which payment method is most associated with churning customers?
- Do senior citizens churn at a higher rate than regular customers?
- Does number of active services affect customer retention?
- Is there any gender-based pattern in churn behavior?
- Which monthly charge group carries the highest churn risk?
- What combination of factors creates the highest-risk customer profile?

Key Findings
- 26.5% overall churn rate across 7,043 customers, with 1,869 customers lost
- Month-to-Month contract holders churn at ~43% vs just 3% for two-year contracts — the single strongest churn predictor
- Fiber Optic customers churn the most (~41%) despite being the premium tier, indicating a price-value gap
- New customers (0–12 months tenure) churn at ~48% — the most critical retention window
- Electronic check payers churn at nearly 2× the rate of auto-pay users
- Senior citizens churn at 41% vs 23% for non-seniors
- Customers with 5+ services show significantly lower churn — cross-selling is a key retention lever
